Socket
  • The ATMEGA328P-PU microcontroller has a DIP package, we have 2 options, solder it directly to the board or use a socket to remove it from the board and put it on a breadboard. In this design we are going to use a socket.
  • This is the only appropriate socket that I have found at Mouser. The manufacturer is Mill-Max and the part number 110-99-328-41-001000
  • It has 28 positions in 2 rows, with pitch 2.54mm and width 7.62mm.
  • In the library Connector_Generic I found the symbol Conn_02x14_Counter_Clockwise
  • I have copied it to the JAVIERM_LIBS library and have added the usual fields in my designs.
  • As I did in lessons 16 and 17 of the KiCad course, I have deleted the pins because the socket is not going to have electrical connections, the microcontroller will.
  • Also it will not have a footprint, so as I did in the KiCad course I associate the footprint that I called null
