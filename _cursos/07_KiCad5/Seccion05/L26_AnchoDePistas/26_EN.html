Track widths
  • Before we start to route the board, let us see how much current a track of a certain width can carry.
  • I am going to use 3 different track widths on the board; 0.25mm , 0.75mm and 1mm .
  • I am going to open the KiCad calculator.
  • Whenever a track carries a certain current, there will be an increase in temperature in the track due to that current.
  • In orange we tell the calculator how much temperature we want the track to increase due to the current, in this case 10ºC. This means that if the ambient temperature is 35ºC the track will be 45ºC.
  • In blue you have to specify how thick (height) the track will be. Normally it is 35um (0.035mm).
  • In red we put the width of the track.
  • The result, in green , is the amount of current the track can carry.
  • The conclusion is that a 0.25mm wide and 35um thick track can drive up to almost 0.9A with a warm-up of only 10ºC.
  • A track 0.75mm wide and 35um thick can drive up to almost 2A with a heating of only 10ºC.
  • A 1mm wide and 35um thick track can drive up to almost 2.4A with a heating of only 10ºC.
