Logo
  • We are going to add a logo on the PCB. KiCad has a bitmap to component converter.
  • I'm going to load the bitmap of my logo.
  • The more resolution the better. I'm going to open the 334x334 pixel image.
  • Select the options to generate a footprint and that the layer where the logo is generated is the silkscreen of the top layer.
  • I want the logo to be 10mm x 10mm
  • You have to calculate the resolution in DPI. The formula is the size in pixels of the original bitmap (334px), divided by the size we want in mm (10mm) and multiplied by 25.4 , which are the mm that an inch has.
  • The resolution is 848
  • Now we have to export.
  • Go to the library directory and give the logo name to the component.
  • We are going to edit our new symbol to the footprint editor.
  • We are going to order the fields of the footprint.
  • We will put the fields of the logo in the F.Fab layer that we do not use in our design.
  • First the Value field.
  • And now the Reference field.
  • Save the footprint.
  • Now we are going to add the footprint on the PCB.
  • Go to the Footprint option of the Place menu.
  • Choose a place on the PCB.
  • And select the logo in our library.
  • Save the PCB.
